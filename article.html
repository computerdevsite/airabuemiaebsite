WHAT IS PLAGIARISM?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, or intellectual property without proper attribution or permission, and presenting it as your own. This can involve copying text, images, or other creative content, but it also includes paraphrasing someone else's ideas without giving credit, or using their research or findings without acknowledgment. Plagiarism is considered unethical and can lead to serious consequences, such as academic penalties, legal action, or damage to one's reputation.

Common types of plagiarism include:

1. Direct Plagiarism: Copying text word-for-word without citation.
2. Mosaic Plagiarism: Borrowing phrases or ideas from a source and mixing them into your own work without proper citation.
3.Paraphrasing Plagiarism: Rewriting someone else's ideas or research in your own words without acknowledging the original source.
4.Self-Plagiarism: Reusing your own previous work without disclosing that it's been used elsewhere.
5. Accidental Plagiarism: Failing to cite sources properly due to lack of understanding or oversight.

To avoid plagiarism, it's essential to always provide proper citations, use quotation marks when directly quoting, and attribute sources when using someone else's work or ideas.

CASES OF PLAGIARISM

COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT

If plagiarism amounts to copyright infringement, it can be a crime under the Intellectual Property Code (IPC). Copyright infringement occurs when someone violates the rights of the copyright owner without their consent.

ONLINE PIRACY

If plagiarism amounts to online piracy, it can be a crime under the E-Commerce Act (Republic Act 8792).

CYBERCRIME

If plagiarism amounts to a violation of the Cybercrime Prevention Act (Republic Act 10175), it can be a crime.

The penalties for plagiarism that amounts to copyright infringement are:

First offense:

•One to three years in prison and a fine of P50,000–P150,000.

Second offense:

•Three years and one day to six years in prison and a fine of P150,000–P500,000.

Third and subsequent offenses:

•Six years and one day to nine years in prison and a fine of P500,000–P1,500,000 .
